Poutine

Description

Poutine's origins trace back to rural Quebec in the late 1950s, where it emerged as a humble and satisfying snack. The dish's exact creation story is debated, with multiple accounts attributing its inception to different restaurants and individuals in the region. One popular narrative suggests that a restaurateur named Fernand Lachance was asked by a customer to combine cheese curds with fries and gravy in a single dish, leading to the birth of poutine. Originally regarded as a simple and inexpensive meal, poutine gradually gained popularity across Canada and internationally, evolving into a beloved comfort food staple that embodies the heartiness and warmth of Canadian cuisine.

Poutine is an indulgence of crispy French fries, savory gravy, and melted cheese curds. Make this delicious French Canadian specialty at home with this easy recipe!

Ingredients

  • Oil: 1 quart vegetable oil for frying
  • Gravy: 1(10.25 ounce)U can beef gravy
  • Fries: 5 medium Russet potatoes (cut into fry shapes)
  • Cheese curds: 2 cups of curd cheese

Steps

Prep: Cut potatoes into 1 inch slices (long way)

  1. Heat oil in a deep fryer or deep heavy skillet to 365 degrees F (185 degrees C).
  2. While the oil is heating, begin to warm gravy.
  3. Place fries into the hot oil, and cook until light brown, 8 to 10 minutes. Cook fries in batches if necessary to allow them room to move a little in the oil. Remove to a paper towel-lined plate to drain.
  4. Place fries on a serving platter, and sprinkle cheese over them.
  5. Ladle warmed gravy over the fries and cheese, and serve immediately.
